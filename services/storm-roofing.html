What the roofing side covers

Our roofing work is storm work. When wind lifts shingles, hail bruises a roof, or a limb punches through the decking, we are the crew that responds. That starts with stopping the damage from growing: an emergency tarp installed the right way, over the ridge and battened down, so the next band of rain stays outside. From there we move to storm damage roof repair, replacing what the weather took and sealing the roof back up so it does its job again.

For bigger events, storm damage restoration ties it all together. A tree across the roofline, a section of decking exposed, gutters torn loose, and debris everywhere is not five separate jobs to five separate companies. It is one storm, and we work it as one project, from the emergency call to the last repair.

Working with your insurance claim

Storm roof damage almost always runs through an insurance claim, and how the first day is handled shapes how the claim goes. We photograph everything before we touch it, because the tarp that protects your house also hides the evidence an adjuster needs. We meet the adjuster on site when it helps, keep an honest record of the scope, and never guarantee an outcome, because every policy and every adjuster reads differently. What we can promise is that your claim will rest on clear documentation instead of memory.

After a big storm, be careful about the crews that appear in the driveway uninvited. The honest move is to choose a local company you can still find next year, and to ask any contractor for proof of insurance and references before the work starts.
