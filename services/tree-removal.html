Tree Removal in Rome, GA

Dead, dying, leaning, or already on the ground: Elite Catastrophe takes trees out safely, in tight yards and over rooftops, and hauls off every last branch before we go.

When a tree needs to come down

Some trees announce it. The canopy thins out one season and never fills back in, bark sloughs off in sheets, mushrooms ring the base, or a lean that used to look charming starts pointing at the kitchen. Others go without warning, snapped or uprooted by a single hard storm. Either way, a dead, dying, or failing tree near anything you care about is a decision waiting to be made, and waiting rarely improves the odds.

We remove trees that have died or turned hazardous, leaning trees that saturated soil has started to tip, storm damaged trees with cracked trunks or hanging limbs, and healthy trees that are simply in the way of a driveway, an addition, or the sunlight your lawn refuses to grow without. If the tree is already down, or already on the house, we handle that too. Fallen trees on structures are exactly why our 24/7 emergency tree service exists.

How Elite Catastrophe works a removal

Every removal starts with eyes on the tree, not a guess from the street. We look at the species and condition, the lean, what sits under and around it, and where the wood can safely go. That assessment decides the plan: a straightforward felling cut where there is open room to lay the tree down, or a climb and rig job where there is not.

Most residential removals around Rome are rigging work. A climber takes the tree apart from the top down, roping limbs and trunk sections so every piece is lowered under control instead of dropped. Over a roof, a fence, or a crowded backyard, that control is the whole job. For big removals, especially large trunks hanging over a house, we bring in crane support and lift sections up and out rather than swinging them over the things they could crush.

Then comes the part too many crews treat as an afterthought: cleanup. Limbs get chipped, trunk wood gets bucked and hauled off, and the lawn gets raked out. We walk the yard with you before we leave. If you want the stump gone too, we can grind it below grade in the same visit.

What shapes the scope of a removal

No two removals take the same crew time, and a few factors drive most of the difference. Size is the obvious one: a towering oak is a different job than an ornamental pear. Access matters just as much, because a tree beside the driveway can be worked quickly while a backyard tree behind a narrow gate comes out piece by piece. Proximity to structures and lines slows everything down on purpose, since close targets mean smaller pieces and more rigging. Condition cuts both ways too: dead wood is brittle and unpredictable, so a long-dead tree often demands a more cautious approach than a healthy one. We look at all of it in person, explain the approach, and put the whole scope in a written free estimate before any saw starts.
