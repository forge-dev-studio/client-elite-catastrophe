Tree Service in Rockmart, Georgia

Spring in Northwest Georgia follows a pattern, and Rockmart homeowners know it by heart. A warm, heavy afternoon, a line of storms building to the west, and then twenty violent minutes that rearrange somebody's yard. In Polk County that usually means pine tops snapped out, sweetgum limbs scattered across driveways, and at least one tree leaning somewhere it was not leaning the day before. Tree trouble here rarely gives much notice, which is why the smart time to deal with a questionable tree in Rockmart is while it is still standing straight. Elite Catastrophe covers Rockmart from our home base in Rome, and we work both sides of town. The older streets near downtown hold mature oaks that have shaded the same houses for generations, trees that mostly need maintenance: deadwood removed, weight reduced, limbs brought back off the roofline. The newer subdivisions have a different problem, the leftover loblolly pine. Those pines grew up inside a stand, sheltered and slender, and once construction took away the trees around them they were left standing alone in heavy clay that turns soft when saturated. A tall, exposed pine in wet ground is the tree most likely to end up horizontal after a windstorm, and it is the one we most wish homeowners would ask about beforehand. Our Rockmart work runs the full list: complete removals of dead, dying, or badly placed trees, careful trimming around roofs and service drops, crown cleaning for the big hardwoods worth keeping, honest hazard assessments when you are just not sure, and stump grinding so a removal ends with a usable yard instead of an obstacle. Rockmart's stretch of the Silver Comet Trail shows how good this corner of Polk County looks when trees are where they belong. When one ends up where it does not belong at two in the morning, Elite Catastrophe answers storm calls 24/7. Either way, you get a family-owned crew that treats your yard like it sits on our own street.
