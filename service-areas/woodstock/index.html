Storm Damage & Emergency Tree Service in Woodstock, GA

Woodstock grew from a small railroad town into one of the largest suburbs in Cherokee County, and the older sections kept their trees the whole way. Heavy hardwood canopy shades the established neighborhoods, while the fast-growing subdivisions around them hold the tall pines that were left behind when construction took away the trees around them. It is a lot of wood hanging over a lot of rooftops, and North Georgia storms know how to find it. When straight-line winds and hail move through, the failures come in a hurry: a loblolly uprooted out of saturated clay and laid across a driveway, an oak limb punched through a garage, a whole tree leaned over onto the power line. That is when Elite Catastrophe gets the call. We are based in Rome, not Woodstock, and we will not pretend otherwise. We are the crew that deploys after the storm, not the one you see around town on a normal Tuesday. Storm response is what we do here, and we do it structures first. When a system rakes across Woodstock, our priority is the trees that have hit homes and vehicles or blocked roads, handled in the order that gets people safe and moving again. Those emergency calls are answered 24/7, through the night and through the weekend, because that is when the wind does its damage. After the emergency passes, we can take care of the rest: removing the trees the storm cracked or killed, trimming the limbs left hanging over the roof, and grinding out stumps once a removal is done. In Woodstock, though, the honest reason to keep our number is simple. When the next line of storms comes through the canopy, you will want a crew already rolling.
