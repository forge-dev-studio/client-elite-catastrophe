Storm Damage & Emergency Tree Service in Holly Springs, GA

Follow I-575 north through Cherokee County and Holly Springs is the growing town on either side of it, filling in with wooded neighborhoods where pines and hardwoods stand tight against new homes. That growth is why storm season matters so much here. Every subdivision carved into the woods leaves tall trees behind, and tall trees in saturated red clay are exactly what North Georgia's straight-line winds go after. The storm math is unforgiving. A loblolly pine that grew up sheltered inside a stand is left tall and exposed once construction takes away its neighbors, and after a week of rain its roots lose their grip in soft clay. One hard gust and it is across the driveway, on the roof, or hung up on the service line. Big oaks fail differently, shedding heavy limbs or splitting at weak unions, but the result on the ground looks the same. Elite Catastrophe answers those calls as a family-owned company based in Rome, serving Holly Springs and the rest of Cherokee County. Storm response is our lead work, run structures first: the trees down on houses, cars, and roads clear before anything else, with emergency service around the clock. When a limb or trunk punches through the roof, we tarp it to hold back the weather until repairs happen. The storm is the headline along the I-575 corridor, but the full list stays available year round: tree removal, tree trimming, stump grinding, and storm damage cleanup once the pressure is off. A tree you have been eyeing since the last blow is worth a look before the next one arrives.
