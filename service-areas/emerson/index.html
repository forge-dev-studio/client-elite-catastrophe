Bartow County

Tree Service in Emerson, Georgia

Growth is the defining fact of Emerson right now, and growth is exactly what changes a town's trees. This small Bartow County town sits near Lake Allatoona and the LakePoint complex along the I-75 corridor, roughly twenty-eight miles from Rome, and it has been filling in fast with new development beside its older lots. That mix is what we see in the yards. Established parcels still hold mature hardwoods, oaks and sweetgums that have shaded the same ground for decades. The newer subdivisions tell a different story: tall loblolly pines left standing after construction stripped away the trees around them, now rooted in compacted clay and suddenly exposed to wind on all sides. A pine that grew up sheltered inside a stand, then lost its neighbors when the lots around it were built, is the single tree most likely to end up flat after a storm, and Emerson has plenty of them. Elite Catastrophe is a family-owned tree service that covers Emerson from our Rome base, bringing the same crew and gear we use in our own neighborhoods. The everyday work here is steady and planned: removing dead, dying, or dangerously placed trees, thinning and cleaning the crowns of hardwoods worth keeping, cutting limbs back off new rooflines, evaluating the leftover pines honestly, and grinding stumps below grade so a new lawn can go in clean. Lakeside lots add their own wrinkle, with damp ground near the water keeping roots loose long after a rain. When storms roll off the lake and across Bartow County, Elite Catastrophe answers emergency calls 24/7. Short of that, the best time to look hard at a questionable pine is now, before the next line of weather does it for you.
