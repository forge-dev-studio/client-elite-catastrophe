Tree Service Across Gordon County

Gordon County wears two faces, and the trees on your property depend on which one your neighborhood belongs to. Calhoun, the county seat, sits right on the I-75 corridor, and it has an established side of in-town streets where water oaks and red oaks have had decades to spread over roofs and power lines, alongside a fast-growing side where new subdivisions keep filling in and the trees are either young plantings or tall pines left over from whatever grew there before. North of Calhoun, Resaca sits quietly on the Oostanaula River amid old battlefield ground, and the smaller communities of Fairmount, Plainville, and Ranger carry the rural canopy of farmhouse hardwoods and roadside pines that Elite Catastrophe knows well.

The problems split along the same lines as the neighborhoods. The old-canopy trouble in Gordon County is the classic kind: big hardwoods holding deadwood over the driveway, water oaks that look full and green while the trunk rots from within, and trees planted a few feet from a foundation back when they were the size of a broomstick. On the newer side, the usual suspect is the leftover loblolly pine, tall and thin and shallow-rooted in red clay, suddenly exposed to wind it never had to stand against while its neighbors surrounded it. After a wet week that clay gives up its grip, and the spring storms do the rest.

We bring the same crew and the same care to Gordon County that we use in our own Rome neighborhoods: removals in tight quarters, precision trimming around roofs and lines, deadwood cleanup in mature crowns, honest hazard evaluations for leaners and storm-scarred trunks, and stump grinding that gives you back a lawn. When severe weather crosses the county, our storm crews respond around the clock. For everything short of that, a calm walk around your property with someone who works on trees every day usually answers the question you have been putting off since the last big blow.

We also cover the smaller communities of Gordon County, including Fairmount, Plainville, Ranger. If you are anywhere in the county, call and tell us where you are.
