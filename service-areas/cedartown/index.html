Tree Service in Cedartown, Georgia

How long has that big oak been standing over your house? In Cedartown, especially on the older streets near downtown, the honest answer is often longer than the house itself. Cedartown is the seat of Polk County, and like most older Georgia county seats it carries a real canopy: red oaks and water oaks over the in-town blocks, sweetgums along the back fences, and stands of loblolly and shortleaf pine ringing the edges of town. Those trees are a big part of why Cedartown feels the way it does. They are also the part of the property most homeowners never think about until something cracks.

Elite Catastrophe is a family-owned tree service based in Rome, and Polk County is a regular part of our week. The work in Cedartown is familiar: mature hardwoods that need weight taken out of the crown before the summer storm season, pines that have browned out and turned brittle, broken limbs hung up over sheds and fences, and stumps left behind by removals that were never truly finished. The clay soil here does trees no favors either. It gets soft after long rain, and a shallow-rooted pine in wet clay is the classic Northwest Georgia blowdown waiting on a gust. Anyone who has walked or ridden the Silver Comet Trail knows how much of Polk County's character comes from its trees.

Our job is to keep the ones on your own property an asset instead of a liability: pruning for structure and clearance, removing hazard trees before they choose their own schedule, and grinding stumps flush so the yard is usable again. When severe weather tears through Polk County, Elite Catastrophe runs storm crews 24/7 until the calls stop coming. The rest of the year, we would much rather look at a tree you are wondering about while it is still standing tall than after it has met your roof.

  • Tree trimming and pruning, for health, clearance, and storm prep
