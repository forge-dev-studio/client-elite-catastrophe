Tree Service in Resaca, Georgia

Along the Oostanaula River in Gordon County, Resaca is a tiny town wrapped in wooded river-bottom land, and that low, damp ground shapes everything about its trees. The river and its floodplain keep the clay soft for weeks at a stretch, which grows big timber but also gives roots a poor grip when the spring storms arrive. About eighteen miles from Rome and split by the I-75 corridor, Resaca has more woods than houses, and many of its trees stand close to homes, barns, and the old battlefield ground that runs through the area. Elite Catastrophe, a family-owned crew, covers Resaca on a regular schedule from our Rome base, and the river-bottom setting turns up a particular kind of tree trouble here. Tall loblolly and shortleaf pines that uproot cleanly when saturated ground lets go. Water oaks along the banks that hollow from the inside while looking healthy up top. Sweetgums and red oaks with heavy limbs stretched out over roofs and outbuildings. Our work in Resaca is the everyday list handled with care: removing hazard trees before the next storm chooses for you, reducing weight in crowns that have outgrown their footing, pruning limbs back off structures, and grinding stumps so a finished removal leaves usable ground. Because so many properties here sit on acreage, we also spend time simply walking the tree line with owners, pointing out which trees near the house are sound and which are quietly declining. When severe weather crosses this stretch of Gordon County, Elite Catastrophe answers storm calls 24/7. The better path is dealing with the leaning riverside pine now, on a calm afternoon, rather than after it has already come down.
