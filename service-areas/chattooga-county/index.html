Tree Service Across Chattooga County

Up in Chattooga County the land begins folding toward the Lookout Mountain foothills, and that terrain changes how trees live and how they come down. Summerville, the county seat, and the mill town of Trion both sit among wooded ridges along the Chattooga River, and wind behaves differently here than it does out in flat country. Storms funnel along the valleys, gusts pour over the ridgelines, and the tall pines and slope-side hardwoods around town take the brunt of it. The smaller communities of Lyerly and Menlo carry the same exposed, wooded character. Anyone who has been through a few spring storm seasons here has watched a healthy-looking tree lose half its crown in a single afternoon.

The species list is familiar Northwest Georgia: loblolly and shortleaf pines that snap or uproot in saturated clay, water oaks that hollow with age, red oaks with heavy horizontal limbs, and sweetgums that drop wood in every stiff wind. What the foothills add is exposure. A tree on a slope or a ridge shoulder catches more wind, anchors in thinner soil, and leans harder as it grows toward the light. That lean is often the first thing a homeowner notices, and the last thing they should ignore, because a tree already leaning over a roof has told you exactly what it plans to do in the next storm.

Elite Catastrophe makes the drive up to Chattooga County regularly, because its homeowners live with some of the most storm-tested trees in our whole working area. Our work here leans toward hazard management for that reason: removing dead and declining trees before they come down on their own terms, reducing weight in crowns that have outgrown their root systems, cutting limbs back off rooflines, and grinding stumps so old removals stop being obstacles in the yard. When the weather turns genuinely dangerous, we field storm calls around the clock. The better plan is the boring one, handling the questionable tree this month rather than on the night the radar turns red.

We also cover the smaller communities of Chattooga County, including Lyerly, Menlo. If you are anywhere in the county, call and tell us where you are.
