Storm Damage & Emergency Tree Service in Ball Ground, GA

Up in the north end of Cherokee County, where the land starts to climb toward the mountains, Ball Ground keeps the feel of a small historic town surrounded by woods. The trees here are a mix of tall pines and mature hardwoods, many of them on wooded acreage and ridge-edge lots where the wind has a clear run at them. That exposure changes how storms play out. When a North Georgia system comes through with straight-line winds and hail, a tree standing out on a slope or an open ridge shoulder catches far more force than one tucked into town. Saturated red clay does the rest, letting a loblolly's roots slip until the whole tree goes over. What lands is the emergency: a pine dropped across the driveway, a hardwood limb through the roof, a trunk leaned onto the power line with the lights out behind it. That is the work Elite Catastrophe brings to Ball Ground. We are based in Rome, roughly forty-seven miles off, and we are straight about it: we are not a local Ball Ground crew, we are the one that deploys when the weather turns. Storm response is the whole point. When severe weather hits north Cherokee, we run structures first, clearing trees off homes, vehicles, and roads ahead of anything else, with emergency calls answered 24/7. Once the hazards are down, we can circle back to the ordinary work: removing what the storm ruined, trimming limbs off the structure, and grinding stumps so the ground is clear again. In a place as wooded and wind-exposed as Ball Ground, though, the storm call is the one that earns its keep.

What we bring to Ball Ground

  • 24/7 emergency tree service, when a storm drops a tree on your home
  • Storm damage restoration, from the tree to the roof it landed on
  • Emergency roof tarping, to stop the water until repairs are made
  • Tree removal, for the hazards a storm leaves standing
