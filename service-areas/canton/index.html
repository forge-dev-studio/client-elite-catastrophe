Storm Damage & Emergency Tree Service in Canton, GA

The pattern is familiar across North Georgia. A hot afternoon, a wall of storms building to the west, and then a violent stretch of wind and hail that leaves Canton homeowners staring at a tree where a tree should not be. As the seat of Cherokee County, sitting on the Etowah River amid wooded hills, Canton mixes a historic downtown with rings of newer subdivisions, and both carry serious tree load. The older streets hold mature oaks that have spread over rooftops for decades. The newer neighborhoods were carved out of pine stands, leaving tall loblollies rooted in red clay that turns soft after a long rain. When the weather hits, those are the trees that fail: pines that snap or uproot, big hardwoods that drop limbs or split, and leaners that end up leaning on a house. Elite Catastrophe is a family-owned company based in Rome, and this is the honest part: we are not a Canton company, and we are not on these streets every day. What we are is storm response. When severe weather crosses Cherokee County, we deploy to Canton and work structures first, meaning the trees that have landed on homes, cars, and roads come before anything cosmetic. Emergency crews answer 24/7, because a tree through the roof at two in the morning cannot wait for business hours. Once the danger is handled, the rest of the work is available as well: removing the trees the storm damaged, trimming limbs back off the roofline, and grinding stumps so the yard is usable again. But in Canton, the call that matters most is the one right after the sky turns green.

  • Storm damage restoration, from the tree to the roof it landed on
  • Emergency roof tarping, to stop the water until repairs are made
