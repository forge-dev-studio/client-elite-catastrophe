Storm Damage & Emergency Tree Service in Dallas, GA

Ask anyone in Dallas who has been through a rough Paulding County storm season and they can describe the aftermath: driveways blocked, limbs down across the yard, and at least one tree leaning somewhere new. As the Paulding County seat, Dallas has grown quickly, its wooded subdivisions spreading across the red clay hills that define this part of Georgia. All that new construction left tall trees standing close to new houses, and that is a setup storms know how to exploit. The clay is the quiet villain. It holds water long after the rain stops, and a loblolly pine rooted in saturated ground has very little to hang onto when straight-line winds arrive. Over it goes, across a driveway or onto a roof. The big hardwoods fail on their own terms, shedding heavy limbs without warning or splitting apart in a strong gust, and either way something on the property usually pays for it. Elite Catastrophe handles Dallas as storm response from our base in Rome, and at roughly thirty miles it is one of the closer towns on our storm map. We will be straight with you: we are not a local Dallas company, and we are not working these streets every day. When severe weather crosses Paulding County, we deploy, and we work structures first, clearing the trees that have landed on homes, vehicles, and roads before anything else. Emergency calls are answered 24/7. When the urgent part is finished, the ordinary work is available too: removing the trees the storm damaged, trimming limbs back off the house, and grinding stumps so the yard is whole again. But in a fast-growing county seat like Dallas, the honest reason to keep our number is the storm and what it leaves in the yard.
