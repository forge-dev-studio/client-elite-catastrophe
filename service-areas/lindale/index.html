Tree Service in Lindale, Georgia

Lindale grew up around its mill, and the old village streets just south of Rome still show it: modest homes set close together, mature shade trees planted long ago, and yards where one big oak can cover half the lot. That closeness is exactly what makes tree work in Lindale different. When a sweetgum is dying between two houses that sit thirty feet apart, there is no room for guesswork. Limbs come down in pieces, on ropes, in the right order, handled by people who do this every day. Elite Catastrophe is a family-owned tree service based just up the road in Rome, which makes Lindale about the shortest trip on our schedule. We know the trees here because they are the same ones we grew up around in Floyd County: water oaks that hollow out with age, sweetgums that shed limbs in summer wind, and tall loblolly pines standing in clay soil that holds water long after the rain stops. When the spring weather turns rough, our crews take storm calls 24/7. Most of what we do in Lindale, though, is planned and scheduled. Removing a dead or declining tree before it picks its own moment to fall. Trimming limbs back off roofs, service drops, and driveways. Grinding out old stumps so you can mow in a straight line again, or finally plant something useful where a tree came down years back. Taking an honest look at a leaner and telling you plainly whether it is a hazard or just a tree with character. If you are in the old village or anywhere on the south side of Floyd County, the process is simple. We walk the yard with you, explain what each tree is doing and why, lay out a clear plan, and leave the place cleaner than we found it. Lindale kept its trees when a lot of towns lost theirs, and helping homeowners live safely under that canopy is work we are glad to have.
