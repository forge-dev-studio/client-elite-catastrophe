Tree Service in Rome, Georgia

Rome sits where the Oostanaula and Etowah meet to form the Coosa, and the same hills and river bottoms that make this town beautiful also make it a serious place to own trees. Elite Catastrophe is based right here in Rome, and Floyd County is our home turf: the historic in-town neighborhoods shaded by oaks that were mature before most of us were born, the ridgetop streets where the wind hits first, and the river-bottom yards where roots sit in damp ground for weeks at a stretch. We climb, trim, and remove trees in this town because we live under the same canopy you do. Older Rome neighborhoods carry a particular kind of tree load. Water oaks and red oaks planted generations ago now stand taller than the houses they shade, with heavy limbs reaching over rooflines, driveways, and sidewalks. Water oaks in particular tend to decline from the inside out, and a trunk that looks solid from the street can be hollow at the base. Add red clay soil that turns greasy after a week of rain, and you get root plates that lose their grip just as the spring thunderstorms arrive. If a leaning pine or a dead limb over the kitchen has been keeping you up at night, that is exactly the call we want to get before the weather makes the decision for you. Our everyday work in Rome is straightforward: full removals in tight yards, careful sectional takedowns where there is no room to drop wood, structural pruning to get limbs off the roof, deadwooding the older oaks worth saving, and stump grinding so the job is actually finished. When severe weather does hit Floyd County, Elite Catastrophe answers storm calls 24/7. Most days, though, the work is quieter than that: keeping Rome's big old trees healthy where they should stay, and taking them down safely where they should not.
