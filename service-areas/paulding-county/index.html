Tree Service & Storm Response Across Paulding County

Paulding County has grown fast, and its wooded subdivisions have grown right along with it, which means more homes tucked under more trees every year. Elite Catastrophe serves the county from our Rome base, and when a North Georgia storm crosses Dallas, the county seat, or the busy US-278 corridor around Hiram, we are set up to respond. The trouble here is the familiar Northwest Georgia pattern intensified by all that new construction: tall loblolly pines left standing when the lots around them were graded, now shallow-rooted in red clay and exposed to wind they never had to face, and large hardwoods dropping heavy limbs on the homes built beneath them. When straight-line winds or hail move through, those are the trees that end up on roofs and across driveways, and our crews work structures first to clear them.

Storm response is where Paulding County homeowners meet us most, but it is not the whole job. The everyday tree work is available year round, not only after a storm: removals of dead, dying, and badly placed trees, trimming to lift limbs off roofs and open crowded crowns, stump grinding so a takedown ends with a lawn instead of an obstacle, and honest evaluations of the leaner or the storm-scarred trunk you have been eyeing. The small community of Braswell and the rural roads between the towns get the same coverage. When a storm opens up a roof, we tarp it to hold the weather out until repairs can follow.

If there is a tree on your Paulding County property that worries you, the calm day is the time to deal with it, while every option is still open and nobody is in a hurry. Call and we will take an honest look. And when the next storm rolls in, you will already know exactly who to reach.

We also cover the smaller communities of Paulding County, including Braswell. If you are anywhere in the county, call and tell us where you are.
